\chap{Abstract}

Cross-sectional single-cell and spatial omics resolve the cellular composition of premalignant tissues but do not directly observe how epithelial states change between ordered disease stages. This thesis introduces StageBridge, a context-conditioned residual transport (CCRT) framework for learning progression-aligned epithelial transition fields while separating the transition expected from the receiver state from the residual effect associated with its local tissue context. StageBridge combines dual-reference latent representations, receiver-centered set-based niche encoding, optimal-transport coupling, and conditional flow matching to model ordered transitions across Normal lung, atypical adenomatous hyperplasia (AAH), adenocarcinoma in situ (AIS), minimally invasive adenocarcinoma (MIA), and invasive lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD).

Applied to a multimodal LUAD precursor cohort spanning single-nucleus RNA sequencing, spatial transcriptomics, and genomic measurements, StageBridge recovered a highly coherent Normal-to-preinvasive transition field and showed that structured local context contributes progression-relevant information beyond the epithelial receiver state alone. Biological analysis identified an early IL1B-associated inflammatory niche, stage-specific immune and stromal remodeling, and a nonlinear sequence of regulatory change. AAH exhibited expanded transcription-factor coordination consistent with inflammatory regulatory priming. The AAH-to-AIS transition showed a marked loss of transcription-factor network organization, identifying a regulatory bottleneck before morphologic invasion. Later progression was characterized by regulatory rewiring together with matrix metalloproteinase and extracellular-matrix remodeling, vascular adaptation, immune suppression, and proliferative expansion.

These findings support a Prime--Collapse--Switch model of lung adenocarcinoma progression in which inflammatory and stromal context first primes a coordinated precursor state, regulatory organization contracts during the transition to AIS, and invasive disease establishes a distinct tissue-supporting program. StageBridge provides a general computational strategy for estimating how local cellular context redirects progression-associated transport from cross-sectional multimodal tissue data.
